BIRINGO WOMEN'S DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION (BIWODA)

Biringo Women Development Association (BIWODA) is a group of rural women who formally collectively came together for a development course in 2014. BIWODA is found in Buramba- Rwemihanga parishes Rubaya- Kahungye Sub Counties, one of the sub counties that make up Kabale District in south western Uganda. (Biringo is a rural area away from the district headquarters [Kabale]. Owing to its remoteness, there are no infrastructures and the social services in general which makes the area one of the most disadvantaged/hard to reach areas in Kabale district.) Women in Biringo community, like any other women in rural Uganda are the bread winners of their families but are the most disadvantaged class of the population with regard to income generation. Families in Biringo community have an average 6 members per family and family daily income is less than 2000 Ugx. These families depend on subsistence farming. Land in Kabale district being scarce, household have limited pieces of land on which they cultivate their crops for home consumption. The women of Biringo are hardworking and resilient but have been trapped in a cycle of poverty for generations. With no access to formal employment, they rely entirely on the land to survive.
