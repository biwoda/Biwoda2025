Rural women are adopting innovative cooking technologies to reduce reliance on firewood and preserve land resources.

Innovations:
  • -Energy-Efficient Stoves: Using less firewood, reducing the pressure on forests.
  • Briquettes from Natural Vegetation like wetlands- Currently we are experimenting anaerobic digestion technology with help from the State University of New York at Buffalo.
  • This innovation will carbonize the organic matter like papyrus reeds. Biringo women want to create fuel briquettes from renewable resources like papyrus reeds, which are abundant and sustainable in Biringo.
